Why Climate Change Matters?

Climate change is not just a distant threat; it is a defining challenge of our time, with far- reaching implications for the health of our ecosystems, the stability of our economies, and the well-being of present and future generations. Rising global temperatures, melting ice caps, extreme weather events, and shifts in ecosystems are just some of the visible signs of a planet in crisis. The concentration of greenhouse gases in the Earth's atmosphere has reached unprecedented levels, leading to rising global temperatures and extreme weather events.

Role of Greenhouse Gases

At the heart of the climate change problem lies the accumulation of greenhouse gases in the Earth's atmosphere, trapping heat and disrupting the delicate balance of our climate system. Through human activities such as burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes, we have significantly increased the concentration of these gases, driving global warming and its associated impacts.

Causes of Climate Change

Fossil fuels – coal, oil and gas – are by far the largest contributor to global climate change, accounting for over 75 per cent of global greenhouse gas emissions and nearly 90 per cent of all carbon dioxide emissions.

Read More

Effects of Climate Change

As greenhouse gas emissions blanket the Earth, they trap the sun’s heat. This leads to global warming and climate change. The world is now warming faster than at any point in recorded history. Warmer temperatures over time are changing weather patterns and disrupting the usual balance of nature. This poses many risks to human beings and all other forms of life on Earth.
